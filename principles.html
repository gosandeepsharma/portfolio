Principles

At a personal level, my goal is to become an exceptionally capable human being — clear in thought, useful in action, adventurous in spirit, and humane in judgment. These are eight principles that guide how I think, act and live.

01

Intellectual humility

Remember that incomplete information and flawed perspective are not exceptions; they are the normal human condition. Acknowledging that is a sign of wisdom, and preferable to loud confidence.

02

Disagreement is good

Disagreement is not hostility; it is respect for the truth. I would rather be challenged than politely misled.

03

Comfort with discomfort

The most useful form of strength is the ability to be unfazed by uncertainty, unfamiliarity and opposition. This comes from resilience, patience and belief in oneself.

04

Systems over symptoms

Don't just fix the leak; fix the root cause. I'll redesign the piping if I have to.

05

Creation over consumption

Life can be lived on a spectrum between consumption and creation. It is one's duty to create, contribute, and move humanity forward. It is one's privilege to experience the improbability and beauty of life on earth.

06

Structure is liberation

Ambiguity is the enemy of decisions. A good roadmap frees attention for the things that actually require judgment, taste, and courage.

07

Taste

Taste is not decoration. It is the ability to know what belongs, what is missing, and what should be removed.

08

Universality

Strive for the reliability of western thought without becoming sterile, and the warmth of eastern thought without becoming vague.

Clean systems, human warmth, and the humility to keep checking my own work.
