About me

I'm a product leader and systems builder with more than fourteen years of experience across engineering, renewable energy, international business and technology. I began my career designing industrial equipment at Forbes Marshall, then worked across Asia-Pacific building partnerships and new markets for renewable-energy company UGE. At Amazon, I have led product initiatives across Prime Video and Cross-Border Retail Tech, working on platform products, customer-experience measurement and complex systems that operate across teams, businesses and geographies. At Prime Video, I spent four and a half years developing ways to make streaming friction observable, measurable and systematically improvable — an experience that deeply shaped how I think about products. I'm now building Alfred, a user-owned identity and context layer for AI. Across these otherwise different chapters, the recurring theme has been the same: taking an ambiguous human or business problem, finding the underlying structure, and turning it into something useful, scalable and thoughtfully made.

Outside the career spine

My interests range across science, history, philosophy, design, technology and sport. They are less separate than they appear: I am interested in how complex systems develop, how people form models of the world, and how an idea can be made both rigorous and useful. Most of my independent projects begin with a question I cannot leave alone. I build because making something is often the fastest way to understand it.
